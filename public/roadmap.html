Platform

Roadmap & direction.

This page captures every major capability we want Instill AI to grow into — from governance and team workflows to webhooks, telemetry, and packaging. Shipped today is the self-hosted preference core (MCP, API, dashboard, billing hooks). Everything else is sequenced honestly below — not vaporware, but not all built yet.

Feedback & summary

Instill wins when preferences are treated as infrastructure, not chat snippets: durable, scoped, reviewable, and observable without becoming generic MCP hosting. The roadmap clusters work into four pillars so we don’t dilute the core story.

  • Governance & lifecycle — Versioning, scopes, lint, semantic search, and team approvals turn rules into policy you can trust in regulated settings.
  • Distribution — Curated bundles and webhooks make onboarding fast and connect preferences to the rest of your stack.
  • Ops & trust — Privacy-preserving telemetry, secret references, and audit exports answer “who changed what” without shipping preference bodies to third parties.
  • Commercial shape — Managed hosting and air-gapped enterprise packaging meet buyers who cannot run raw Node/SQLite themselves or cannot touch the public internet.

Shipped core · v2 baseline

What you get when you deploy the current open distribution — the foundation every other item extends.

MCP preference tools

Live

Streamable HTTP MCP with save, list, get, update, delete, search, category filters, and session reset — preferences injected across assistants.

Outcome · One authenticated endpoint for Cursor, Claude, and compatible clients.

REST & OpenAPI

Live

Dashboard-backed CRUD plus OpenAPI for ChatGPT Actions and custom integrations.

Outcome · Same rules for MCP-native and HTTP-only stacks.

Categories & keyword search

Live

Group rules by category; SQLite-backed text search today — semantic upgrades listed separately.

Outcome · Organise large libraries before full semantic layers land.

Teams & admin

Live

Per-user isolated stores, optional admin panel, API key rotation, Stripe plan hooks for paid tiers.

Outcome · Shared deployment without shared preference text.

Governance & lifecycle

Turn flat rules into managed policy: history, environment boundaries, quality gates, and structured collaboration.

Versioning & rollback

Next

Immutable snapshots per preference or workspace, diff view, restore to any point in time; optional named releases.

Outcome · Safe iteration when rules drive production behaviour.

Scopes & environments

Next

Bind rules to repo, project, client, or env (dev/staging/prod) so get_preferences resolves context automatically.

Outcome · Same mental model as feature flags — fewer conflicting globals.

Rule lint & CI checks

Planned

Validate packs for conflicts, unsafe instructions, and contradictions; CLI + optional MCP tool for pre-merge gates.

Outcome · Catch bad rules before they reach assistants.

Semantic search & clustering

Planned

Embeddings over rule text for “what applies here?” and duplicate detection; optional local model or bring-your-own endpoint.

Outcome · Large org libraries stay searchable without hand-tagging everything.

Team workflow & audit

Planned

Draft → review → approve for Business-tier governance; full audit trail of who changed what and when (ties to versioning).

Outcome · Defensible changes for compliance-heavy teams.

Distribution & integrations

Ship templates faster and notify downstream systems when preferences move.

Policy bundles marketplace

Planned

Curated starter packs (security, accessibility, stack-specific); start free templates, optional verified bundles later.

Outcome · Minutes to value for new workspaces.

Webhooks & event stream

Planned

preference.created|updated|deleted to HTTPS, Slack, or queue adapters — sync wikis, tickets, or internal ops without polling.

Outcome · Preferences participate in org workflows.

Ops & trust

Operate with confidence: observe health without leaking content; keep secrets out of free-text rules.

Usage & health telemetry

Planned

Aggregated metrics only — tool call counts, latency buckets, error rates, storage growth. No rule body leaves the deployment unless you opt in.

Outcome · Admins justify spend and debug MCP paths safely.

Secrets separation

Planned

Reference secrets by key from env, Vault, or Doppler-style providers — preferences hold pointers, not tokens.

Outcome · Rotation and audit without grep-ing rule text for keys.

Audit exports API

Planned

Machine-readable export for SIEM and retention — extends team audit trail with scheduled dumps.

Outcome · Fits enterprise evidence requests without bespoke consulting.

Commercial packaging

Reach buyers who won’t self-host raw Node — without abandoning the open core.

Managed hosting tier

Planned

Same binaries, operator-run: backups, upgrades, TLS — optional separate SKU from self-host software.

Outcome · Revenue from teams that need uptime without owning the metal.

Air-gapped / enterprise bundle

Planned

Offline docs, SAML-forward packaging, hardening guides, and install artefacts for environments without outbound internet.

Outcome · Sell into regulated networks where SaaS defaults fail.

Scope & expectations

Implementing the full surface above is a multi-release programme, not a single pull request. Priority order will follow customer demand: teams blocked on governance pull versioning and approvals forward; platform teams pull webhooks and telemetry; regulated buyers pull audit and air-gap packaging. Watch this page and release notes as items graduate from Planned → Next → Live.
